## Structured note
# Fault Models

[figure]

Timing faults don't really apply for asynchronous network models, where messages take arbitrarily long to deliver.

Omission faults and crash faults are the main subjects in this class

[figure]

Authentication detectable Byzantine faults can be downgraded to omission faults (drop the messages)

- ☆ crash model = crash faults may occur
- ☆ omission model = omission and crash faults may occur

In the omission fault model, two participants can't reach an agreement (two general's)

"common knowledge" solves this
  - everyone knows P
  - everyone knows that everyone knows P
  - everyone knows ... everyone knows P
    - infinite
