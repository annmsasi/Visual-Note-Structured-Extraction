## Structured note

## LC algorithm

1. Every process will keep a counter, initially 0.
2. On every event, a process increments it's counter by 1.
3. When sending a message, a process includes its counter with the message.
4. When receiving a message, set your clock to $[\max(\text{local clock}, \text{received clock})+1]$

- The Lamport clock does not guarentee a happens before relation (look at nodes E&G) (inverse clock condition)
  - If $LC(A) < LC(B)$, this doesn't mean $A \to B$. 

$$e_1 \to e_2 \implies LC(e_1) < LC(e_2)$$

contrapositive:

$$LC(e_1) \geq LC(e_2) \implies \neg(e_2 \to e_1) \lor (e_1 \parallel e_2)$$

### Vector Clocks

Vector Clocks guarentee inverse clock condition

1. Every process maintains a vector of natural numbers, initialized to 0. The length of the vector is the number of processes.
2. On every event, a process increments its own position in the clock
