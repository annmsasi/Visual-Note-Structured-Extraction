# CSE 138 Lecture #2

## Why is time useful? Coordination

How do we use clocks?

1. Mark particular instances in time
2. Measure durations or intervals of time

### Physical

Computers have 2 clocks:

- **Monotonic clocks**
  - only go forward
  - bad for timestamping particular points in time
  - good for measuring durations / intervals of time
- **Time-of-day clocks**
  - synchronized between machines using NTP (Network Time Protocol)
  - bad for measuring durations / intervals of time
    - because they can jump backwards and forwards (2016 Cloudflare leap second bug)
  - Ok (ish) for timestamping points in time

Book Rev. Designing Data Intensive Applications

why?

Any out of sync clocks can lead to confusion of ordering

### Logical clocks

Only measure the ordering of events

$$A \rightarrow B : \text{A happened before B}$$

- A could have caused B, B could not have caused A
