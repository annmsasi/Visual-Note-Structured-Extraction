## Reliable broadcast:

- Problem: we could crash between unique messages. We want the broadcast to be all or noone
  - If a process (which hasn't crashed) delivers a message, then all other (correct) processes deliver that message

If a process crashes before it can deliver a message then this doesn't violate reliable broadcast.

If a process crashes in the middle of a broadcast, does violate reliable delivery.

### How to implement?

- Upon receiving a message, broadcast to every other process, before delivering
  - except the process which sent (optimization)
- could result in duplicate messages in reliable broadcast (also called atomic broadcast)

> fault tolerance often involves making copies
